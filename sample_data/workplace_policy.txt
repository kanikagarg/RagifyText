**Workplace Behaviour Policy Document**

---

**Purpose**:
The purpose of this Workplace Behaviour Policy is to establish clear expectations regarding conduct and interactions within ACompany, in order to create a safe, respectful, inclusive, and productive work environment for all employees, contractors, vendors, and visitors.

**Scope**:
This policy applies to all employees of ACompany, including full-time, part-time, temporary staff, interns, contractors, and remote workers, across all global offices and remote settings.

---

### 1. Expected Standards of Behaviour

All employees are expected to:

* Treat others with dignity, respect, fairness, and courtesy.
* Communicate professionally and constructively at all times.
* Value diversity and promote an inclusive work culture.
* Foster collaboration, teamwork, and mutual support.
* Maintain integrity and honesty in all professional interactions.
* Uphold the company's code of ethics and compliance standards.

These standards are essential to maintain a workplace that reflects ACompanyâ€™s values and legal responsibilities. Compliance with these behavioural expectations ensures that every employee can thrive in an environment free from harassment, discrimination, and conflict. It promotes not only trust and transparency within teams but also protects the organization from reputational and legal risks. By adhering to these principles, employees contribute to a culture of mutual respect and high ethical standards that benefits both individuals and the organization as a whole.
---

### 2. Harassment, Discrimination & Bullying

ACompany has a **zero-tolerance policy** for any form of:

* **Harassment**: Unwelcome verbal, physical, or visual conduct that creates an intimidating or hostile work environment.
* **Discrimination**: Unfair treatment based on race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, nationality, or any protected characteristic.
* **Bullying**: Repeated, unreasonable behaviour that degrades, intimidates, or isolates individuals.

Examples of prohibited behaviour include:

* Derogatory remarks or jokes
* Unwanted physical contact or advances
* Threats, yelling, or verbal abuse
* Cyberbullying or exclusion in online collaboration spaces
* Retaliation for reporting inappropriate conduct

Maintaining a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and bullying is fundamental to ensuring the psychological safety and well-being of all employees. These behaviors are not only unethical, but they also undermine team cohesion, reduce productivity, and expose the company to serious legal liabilities. ACompany is committed to fostering a professional environment where everyone feels valued and supported, and any breach of this commitment will be taken seriously and addressed promptly.
---

### 3. Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI)

ACompany is committed to:

* Actively promoting equal opportunity in hiring, promotion, and training.
* Ensuring representation across all levels of the organization.
* Supporting employee resource groups (ERGs) and diversity initiatives.
* Providing accommodations for individuals with disabilities.

Employees are encouraged to:

* Recognize and challenge unconscious biases.
* Participate in DEI training and workshops.
* Respect cultural and identity differences in communication and collaboration.

---

### 4. Workplace Relationships

**4.1 Personal Relationships**

* Personal relationships between employees (including romantic or familial) are permitted but must not interfere with work performance or team dynamics.
* Relationships between employees in a direct reporting line **must be disclosed** to HR to prevent conflicts of interest.
* In such cases, reporting structures may be adjusted to maintain objectivity.

**4.2 Manager-Employee Relationships**

* Romantic relationships between a manager and a direct or indirect report are strongly discouraged.
* If such a relationship occurs, it must be immediately disclosed to HR. Failure to do so may result in disciplinary action.

**4.3 Professional Boundaries**

* All employees are expected to maintain professional boundaries in the workplace, even when personal relationships exist.
* Public displays of affection, favoritism, or biased decision-making will not be tolerated.

---

### 5. Workplace Conduct in Virtual Environments

The same behavioural standards apply to virtual workspaces, including:

* Company messaging platforms (e.g., Slack, Teams)
* Video calls and virtual meetings
* Email and collaborative tools (e.g., Docs, Notion, Jira)

Employees should:

* Avoid sending inappropriate or sarcastic messages
* Be mindful of tone in written communications
* Respect time zones and working hours
* Keep video meeting environments professional

---

### 6. Alcohol, Drugs, and Workplace Conduct

* The use of illegal drugs or being under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance while at work or representing the company is strictly prohibited.
* Alcohol may be consumed at approved company events in moderation.
* Employees found violating this policy may face immediate disciplinary action, including termination.

---

### 7. Dress Code and Personal Presentation

* ACompany encourages a smart casual dress code that reflects professionalism and respect for colleagues and clients.
* Employees attending client meetings or in-person events must dress appropriately to represent the company brand.
* Remote employees should be camera-ready for meetings unless otherwise exempted.

---

### 8. Reporting and Investigations

If an employee witnesses or experiences inappropriate behaviour:

* Report the incident to HR, your manager, or anonymously via the Ethics Hotline.
* Reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly.
* Retaliation against individuals who report misconduct in good faith is strictly prohibited.

All complaints will be:

* Taken seriously
* Investigated thoroughly by HR or a designated third-party
* Resolved based on factual findings and company policy

---

### 9. Consequences of Misconduct

Violations of this policy may result in:

* Verbal or written warning
* Mandatory training or counseling
* Suspension or demotion
* Termination of employment
* Legal action in cases of severe violations

Each case will be evaluated on its merits, severity, and intent, and may involve consultation with legal and compliance teams.

---

### 10. Employee Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for:

* Reading and understanding this policy
* Completing mandatory workplace behaviour training
* Acting as role models for respectful conduct
* Speaking up against inappropriate behaviour

Managers have additional responsibilities to:

* Lead by example
* Address behavioural issues promptly
* Support team well-being and inclusivity

---

### 11. Policy Review and Updates

This policy will be reviewed annually by the People Operations team. Employees will be notified of any material changes and are required to acknowledge the updated policy.
